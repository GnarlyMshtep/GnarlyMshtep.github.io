Matan Shtepel

Hello! I am an undergraduate at UCLA, studying computer science and mathematics, and currently, I am being advised by Prof. Rafail Ostrovsky and collaborating with Jacob Zhang. In particular, we are working in Multi-Party Computation (MPC) with an emphasis on Distributed Oblivious RAM protocols. Additionally, I organize the UCLA TCS Guild. You can find my CV here.

Previously, I collaborated with Dr. Mark Burgin on the axiomatic theory of algorithms, our joint paper, "On Totalization of Computable Functions in a Distributive Environment" was recently accepted! Further back, I worked with Dr. William Pezzaglia on quaternion graphics.

When not pondering abstract problem in and out the standard curriculum or building subpar academic websites, I enjoy collaborating on various software projects, reading (now reading Quantum Computing since Democritus and it's awesome!), goofing-around with fashion, exercising decently often, badly surfing, mediocrely snowboarding, and spending an unhealthy amount of time on looking at mathematician and scientists' bios on Wikipedia. Next year, I intend to apply for graduate school and pursue a PhD in theoretical computer science.
